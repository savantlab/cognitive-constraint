## Executive Summary

We're building a **dual-entity structure** (non-profit Foundation + for-profit Company) to disrupt psychological science by:
1. Publishing peer-reviewed invalidations of flawed cognitive models (mental rotation, IQ testing, cognitive complexity)
2. Paying researchers for quality peer review and validated publications
3. Using exclusive institutional subscriptions to create competitive pressure for reform
4. Commercializing validated data and expert services

**Key innovations:**
- Pay reviewers $500-5K, authors $5K-100K (breaks academic conformity incentives)
- Exclusive university subscriptions ($500K-5M/year) with citation rights
- Mimetic theory framework (Girard) - IQ testing as scapegoating mechanism
- Training data products for AI companies (LLMs trained on pseudoscience problem)

## The Scarcity/Exclusivity Strategy (Game-Changing)

### **Key Insight: Control Access to the New Paradigm**

**Not open access journals - this is access-controlled paradigm shift:**

**Citation restrictions:**
- Papers readable by anyone (SEO, discoverability)
- **But can only be cited if institution subscribes**
- After IQ invalidation, can't publish in intelligence research without citing it
- Schools without subscriptions publish obsolete work

**Institutional tiers:**

**Observer ($25K/year):**
- Can read papers
- Cannot cite in publications
- Cannot submit or peer review
- "Pay to watch the revolution without you"

**Participant ($100-250K/year):**
- Can cite papers
- Eligible for paid peer review
- Cannot submit papers yet
- Must show curriculum reforms first

**Publisher ($500K-5M/year):**
- Full access: cite, review, publish
- Priority review
- Faculty get paid for papers ($5-100K each)

- Must prove: curriculum changes, public commitment to standards, removal of invalidated theories

## Payment Structure (Radical Innovation)

### **Paying for Quality, Not Conformity**

**Peer reviewers:**
- Simple review: $500 (4-8 hours)
- Computational replication: $1,500 (8-16 hours)
- Independent verification: $2,000-5,000 (20+ hours)
- Refutation bounty: $10,000+ (if you successfully invalidate published paper)
- **Key:** Paid same whether approve or reject (no rubber-stamping incentive)

**Authors:**
- Replication study: $5,000
- Novel invalidation: $15,000-25,000
- Paradigm-shift (IQ-level): $50,000-100,000
- Impact bonuses (6-12 months later):
  - 100+ citations: $5,000
  - 500+ citations: $20,000
  - Institutional adoption: $10-50K
  - Policy change: $50-200K
  - Commercial licensing: 5-15% royalties

**Example: IQ paper author trajectory:**
- Base payment: $75,000 (immediate)
- Year 1 impact bonus: $50,000 (citations, media)
- Years 2-5 royalties: $50-500K (training data licensing, expert witness revenue)
- **Total potential: $175K-$625K for one paper**

**Why this works:**
- Academics make $90-150K/year
- One paper = 1-4 years' salary
- Financial incentive > mimetic conformity pressure
- Career pseudoscientists flip sides (it's stupid not to)

## Mimetic Theory Framework (Imitatio Grant Angle)

### **Girard's Theory Applied to Psychology**

**Core concepts:**
- Human desire is mimetic (imitative), not autonomous
- Scapegoating mechanisms resolve mimetic crises
- Mimetic rivalry drives institutional behavior

**How this explains psychology's problems:**

**1. IQ Testing = Literal Scapegoating**
- "Low IQ" individuals designated as scapegoats
- Death penalty threshold (Atkins v. Virginia)
- Justifies exclusion from opportunities
- Tests mathematically flawed but mimetically adopted (everyone copies)

**2. Academic Mimesis**
- Researchers imitate established theories (tenure survival)
- Peer review mimics prior judgments (conformity)
- Universities copy each other's curricula (mimetic rivalry)
- Invalid models persist through mimesis, not evidence

**3. Paradigm Shift as Mimetic Crisis**
- Invalidations cause field-wide crisis (what do we believe now?)
- Institutions resist (defending mimetic order)
- Your exclusive subscriptions leverage mimetic competition (Princeton vs. Caltech rivalry)
- Cascade effect: Everyone must subscribe or face mimetic humiliation

**4. Surrealism and Mimetic Perception**
- Surrealism exposes how perception is culturally imitated
- Cognitive models assume "natural" perception (ignore mimesis)
- Your art technique generates data outside mimetic frameworks
